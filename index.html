Working Papers

The serial dependence in commodity futures markets, with Yufeng Han

  • Presentation: 5th Annual J.P. Morgan Center for Commodities (JPMCC) International Symposium (8/15/2022); 2021 Miami Herbert Business School Winter Research Conference on Machine Learning and Business (2/12/2021); 2020 Financial Management Association (FMA) Annual Meeting(10/20/2020)
  • Examine the lead-lag relations of commodity futures using machine learning models (multiple LASSO models, gradient boosting, random forests and neural networks) and build trading strategies
  • Build a profitable single-sort portfolio (average annualized excess return 15.15%, Sharpe 0.93, alpha 16.61%)
  • Identify that indexed trading caused by financialization explains the profitability of the lagged returns

Information Risk and Stock Returns of Companies Going Public By Merging with SPACs , with Al Ghosh and Brian Burnett See Paper

  • Presentation: 2022 American Accounting Association (AAA) Annual Meeting (8/3/2022)
  • Validate the proposed SEC 2022 rules that aim to improving the relevance, completeness, clarity, and comparability of the disclosures provided by SPACs (Special Purpose Acquisition Companies) at the initial public offering and de-SPAC transaction stages.
  • Find that that the information risk (measured by financial statement quality, stock exchange choice, and delisting activities) is about 71% higher for SPAC-IPOs than for non-SPAC-IPOs.
  • Find that the information risk persists three years into the SPAC-IPO’s public listing
  • Demonstrate that the variation in the information risk explains the poor stock performance of SPAC-IPOs.
