Hi, I am a postdoctoral research associate and instructor at Olin School of Business, Washington University in St Louis. I have multiple areas of expertise, including empirical asset pricing, investment, corporate finance, financial institutions, risk management, and fintech. I share my research work on SSRN.

Research

My research aims to better understand fundamental questions in the financial market such as the followings:

  • What are the common risk factors in stock and derivatives markets?
  • How can we improve the predictability of expected returns and volatilities in stock and derivatives markets?
  • How do managerial behaviors and firm policies affect stock returns?
  • How to reduce the conflicts of interest between shareholders and managers in publicly traded firms?

Working Papers

The Lead-Lag Relations in Commodity Futures Markets, with Yufeng Han

  • Presentation: 2022 Southern Finance Association (SFA) Annual Meeting (11/19/2022); 5th Annual J.P. Morgan Center for Commodities (JPMCC) International Symposium (8/15/2022); 2021 Miami Herbert Business School Winter Research Conference on Machine Learning and Business (2/12/2021); 2020 Financial Management Association (FMA) Annual Meeting(10/20/2020)
  • Examine the lead-lag relations in commodity futures using machine learning models (multiple LASSO models, gradient boosting, random forests and neural networks) and build trading strategies
  • Build a profitable single-sort portfolio (average annualized excess return 15.15%, Sharpe 0.93, alpha 16.61%)
  • Identify that indexed trading caused by financialization explains the profitability of the lagged returns

Information Risk and Stock Returns of Companies Going Public By Merging with SPACs, with Al Ghosh and Brian Burnett

  • Presentation: 2022 American Accounting Association (AAA) Annual Meeting (8/3/2022)
  • Examine the information risk (measured by financial statement quality, auditor choice, earnings persistence, stock exchange choice, and size) and stock returns for companies going public by merging with Special Purpose Acquisition companies (“SPAC-IPOs”)
  • Find that the information risk persists two years into the SPAC-IPO’s public listing
  • Demonstrate that the variation in the information risk causes the poor stock performance of SPAC-IPOs
  • Provide empirical support to the proposed SEC 2022 rules on SPACs
